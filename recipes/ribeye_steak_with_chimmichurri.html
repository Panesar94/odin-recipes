Ribeye Steak with Chimmichurri

Description

Ribeye is the king of all steaks! Here we'll go over the technique used to cook it and prepare the famous Argentinian salsa known as chimmichurri!

Ingredients

  • 1 12OZ bone in ribeye steak with good marbling
  • 1 Tablespoon neutral oil such as avocado oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste, preferably sea salt and fresh ground pepper
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 4-5 sprigs of thyme
  • 1/4-1/2 cup each cilantro, parsley, oregano
  • Juice of 1/2 a fresh lemon and its zest
  • 2-3 red thai chillies, or more/less depending on your preference
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil

Directions

  1. Take steak out and allow to come to room temperature for at least 1 hour before cooking
  2. Combine all herbs excluding thyme, 3 cloves of garlic, lemon peel and juice, red wine vinegar, salt and pepper in a blender or food processor
  3. Blend ingredients together until fairly smooth, depending on personal preference you can leave it a bit more chunky
  4. While pulsing/running on low slowly stream in olive oil till everthing is fully combined
  5. Taste for seasoning, if needed add more salt and pepper then transfer to a bowl and put it in the fridge to let the flavours incorporate
  6. Generously salt and pepper all sides of the steak
  7. Heat a cast iron, stainless steel or carbon steel pan on high heat, just till faint whisps of smoke appear
  8. Put approximately 1 tablespoon of avocado oil in the pan and gently let it spread out, then place the steak in the pan and slightly press it down
  9. Lower heat to medium and allow steak to develop a crust
  10. Allow steak to cook for 3 minuets before flipping and cooking another 2 minuets
  11. Flip steak onto fat side and allow it to render for about 90 seconds
  12. Add in remaining cloves of garlic and thyme along with butter and put heat on low after butter melts and foams
  13. Flip steak back down and continuosly baste steak with butter using a spoon for about 1 min
  14. Remove steak to a plate and allow to rest for five minuets to allow the juices in the steak to redistribuite,add the butter, herbs and garlic from the pan on top
  15. Once rested put steak on a warm plate and serve with the chimmichurri on the side

And thats all there is to make an amazing steak at home! You can always try different seasonings for the steak like using differnet types of peppercorns, chilli peppers, and things such as granulated garlic or onion. Its important to try different things to figure out what you like and what works together! In addition you could also serve the steak by cutting it off the bone, slicing it against the grain and topping it with the chimmichurri. You can also experiment with different herbs and acids even different chilli peppers for the chimmichurri. Different acids and chillis will give different flavour profiles!
